FAQ

How long is the construction of a modular house?

Building a modular house typically takes 1 to 3 months, depending on its size and specifications.

Which materials are used in construction?

Modular houses use a variety of materials, with the primary structure being a metal or wooden frame. Walls and floors are often made from OSB boards, SIP panels, or sandwich panels filled with polystyrene or other insulation materials.

Can the layout be customized?

Yes, modular house layouts can be customized both during the design stage and after construction. You can order a personalized layout before building, and make interior modifications after completion.

Can you live in a modular house during winter?

Yes, you can safely live in a modular house during winter, provided it is properly insulated and equipped with a reliable heating system.

What factors affect the cost of a house?

The price of a modular house depends on its size, project complexity, chosen materials (for both the structure and interior), insulation level, configuration, and engineering systems (water, heating, electricity, etc.). Optional features like plumbing, air conditioning, or landscaping can also raise the final cost.
